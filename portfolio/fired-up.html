Fired Up: A Presentation for the Mint Museum

Environmental Graphic Designer
Project Details
  • Client: The Mint Museum
  • Location: Charlotte, North Carolina
  • Date: June 2016

The Brief

As a presentation to the Mint Museum of Charlotte, North Carolina, I developed a sample graphics treatment for the exhibition Fired Up: Contemporary Glass by Women Artists from the Toledo Museum of Art. It includes a graphic visual and text element. Together they represent the heat of creating glass while utilizing the reflective qualities of the paint–the same way glass might. This design is represented inside the exhibition using colored light and in print through an overlay of transparent color.

About The Client

The Mint Museum is a leading, innovative museum of international art and design committed to engaging and inspiring all members of our global community. The Mint Museum is dedicated to leadership in collecting, exhibiting, conserving, researching, publishing, interpreting, and sharing art and design from around the world. These commitments are central to the museum’s core values of leadership, integrity, inclusiveness, knowledge, stewardship, and innovation, promoting understanding of and respect for diverse peoples and cultures. Through our two locations, Mint Museum Uptown and Mint Museum Randolph , we hope to stimulate the senses, ignite inner passion and compel our visitors to share the things that inspire them with the world. Just as our artists do.
